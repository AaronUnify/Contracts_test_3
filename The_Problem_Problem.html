The Problem Problem

Life is great, except for problems.



If you're alive you have problems. Problems disrupt our experience by generating negative emotion. Excessive negative emotion can block our ability to live a meaningful life. Forcing us onto a path that leads to resentment, regret and bitter suffering.

Fortunately individual problems can be solved reducing our suffering and keeping us on a meaningful path through life, but in time new problems emerge. The problem of problems is unending and should not be ignored. Too many problems will complicate your life to the point of collapse*.

The current imperfections of the world are the generators of future problems. I wish to acknowledge that societies will always be plagued by evil people who exploite others' vulnerabilities. I would like to talk more about solutions to problems caused by evil in future articles. For now I want to talk about two of the biggest causes of problems in the world, scarcity and uncertainty. Scarcity and uncertainty exist independent of evil at every level of existence and continually generate problems for people.

I would like to focus on scarcity and uncertainty because they have partial answers. It may not be possible to erase scarcity and uncertainty, but it is possible to reduce them. To reduce scarcity become more efficient, to reduce uncertainty adopt superior strategy.

Choosing to filter your decision making through a lense of efficiency and strategy gives a framework for evaluating your own actions. The goal of efficiency is to do more with what you have, the goal of strategy is eliminate potential negative outcomes while conserving expected and better than expected outcomes. "If your experience consistently generates anxiety and depression, you may be building a life that isn't right for you." The beauty of strategy is that it can be updated in response to worse than expected outcomes. Your strategy should be chosen and tailored to your personal situation by you. The times where your decision making has resulted in a sub-optimal outcome are the times to reflect on your chosen strategy. Always at least with the minimum goal of not making the same mistake twice.

Integrating efficiency and strategy into your plans can be done on every level of decision making. Your 5-year plan should be strategic and efficient as should your 1-week plan. Your space should be optimised for your goals and your goals should be optimised to your resources.

Each successful decision increases the probability of future successful decisions. Each worse than expected outcome is an opportunity to recalibrate your strategy to increase the probability of future successful decisions.

Once you begin to grapple with the problem of problems your strategy can be narrowed to key areas. Adopt specific strategies to solve the meaning problem, the money problem, the relationship problem ect. There are several categories of life where problems will continually emerge but it is important to put meaning at the forefront of your experience. If experience does not feel meaningful, then you may be building a life that isn't right for you.

*If you feel overwhelmed by problems, I recommend starting a Unify account to begin untangling them. However, if you are unsure where to begin feel free to continue reading.
