Maybe social media is not the collective beliefs of the people. Maybe there is independence to its actions. If we're lucky, figuring out its nature may provide insight into ourselves. I started by assuming the left was public belief and the right was private belief. But that can't possibly be all there is to what's happening here. That initial concept seems childish now, I kind of wish I didn't start unpacking these ideas, but I'll go as far as I can.

The mind and social media are related in that they both exist in the information realm. The mind uses processed memories and unprocessed generalities when simulating the world. The mind is simulating the world for the purpose of prediction. Social media uses an attention-seeking algorithm to lure people down slithers of a secondary reality. These secondary realities are not predictive, just projections of one part of a user's self. Whatever it is that your timeline shows, is an artificially generated reflection of your current interests and history, even if you don't know it.

Of course, compassionate and caring people do actually dominate the public discussion on these platforms because their opinions are safer to share publicly than most. But any level of competition is enough to bring the competitive people to the party. They begin to post increasingly compassionate content in an attempt to outdo one another. This leads to an unconscious resentment building in the crowd. They sense the scales have been tipped from the truth. After all, we can't protest every tree getting chopped down, sometimes trees are just in the way.

In comes a helpful person to tip public discourse back to truth, but oh no! They used too much pressure. Now everyone's upset and they need to make an apology. Meanwhile, the compassion competition continues uninterrupted because no one gets asked to apologize for compassion.

This process will go on until all the causes with the good victim groups are taken. Hopefully, after that, it'll taper off, because a competitive push to the frontiers of compassion to find groups to be compassionate towards is a dangerous game long-term. Eventually, the world will turn into a landscape of identity either sanctioned or unsanctioned by social media. Each time the mass of people agree a small underbelly of disagreers emerges. Instead of posting publicly the heretics message in private chats, slowly building trust and developing their ideas. Trust is required to form mutual expression of opinion when that opinion could be controversial. Once an underbelly of disagreers is at the point of generating memes the building blocks of their ideas are shared amongst increasing numbers of people using humor to make the unpalatable idea palatable.

Slowly in the social media timeline, these counter-ideas start to appear tentatively. Eventually, someone takes it too far, and boom! Massive apology in order, this can be a devastating blow to some careers. But strangely not others.
